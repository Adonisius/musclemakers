Introduction

Going to the gym could be a stressful, confusing and overwhelming experience for beginners. Intimidating workout routines, potentially strict diets, and just a general life change could be more than stressful on a newcomer. Our aim is to provide information on muscle groups, dietary information and just general tips to help make the transition into a healthier lifestyle a smooth and welcome experience. Here is a list of different muscle groups, each including their own descriptions and appropriate workouts for each. This website provides simple, easy-to-do and home-friendly exercises that push anyone and everyone in the right direction. These exercises include links to YouTube videos, showing how anyone can do these exercises if there is any confusion. Some exercises require some equipment, but most exercises are equipment-free and available to do whenever. Here are some quick navigation tools for anyone looking to jump in to a specific muscle group!

Biceps

The biceps or biceps brachii is a large muscle that lies on the front of the upper arm between the shoulder and the elbow. Both heads of the muscle arise on the scapula and join to form a single muscle belly which is attached to the upper forearm. These exercises require equipment, but you could improvise just by using a heavy bag or filling up a milk carton with water. There is plenty of creative solutions, and many moer creative exercises!

  • Bicep Curls
  • Hammer Curls
  • Incline Hammer Curls
  • Cross Body Hammer Curls
  • Cross Body Hammer Curls

    • Chest

    The chest is a part of the anatomy of humans and various other animals located between the neck and the abdomen. Most chest exercises require equipment, as a replacement different types of push-up techniques have been listed that tackle different things. They are compound exercises, and can be used in a variety of different ways to fulfill whatever goals you have set out for yourself!

  • The Perfect Push Up
  • Incline Push Up
  • Wide Push Up
  • Wall Push Up
  • Diamond Push Up

    • Shoulders

    The human shoulder is made up of three bones: the clavicle (collarbone), the scapula (shoulder blade), and the humerus (upper arm bone) as well as associated muscles, ligaments and tendons. With a plethora of exercises, there is plenty of room to match and experiment with what works best for you!

  • Plank Raise Tap Crunch
  • Sit-Through
  • Diving Dolphin
  • Dumbbell Lateral Raise
  • Military Press

    • Back and Lats

    Large posterior area of the human body, rising from the top of the buttocks to the back of the neck. With the back being an extensive muscle group requiring different specific exercises to tackle certain muscles, it is imperative to mix and match different exercises to ensure the back grows as one big muscle rather than focusin gon one specific group.

  • Single-Arm Dumbbell Rows
  • Plank with Lateral Arm Raise
  • Push-Up Hold
  • Back and Booty Blasters
  • Pilates Press

    • Legs

    A leg is a weight-bearing and locomotive anatomical structure. Our legs are used for essentially everything on a day to day basis; going to work or the kitchen, even getting out of bed. Our legs have the largest muscle groups in the human body, so be reassured to find some very entertaining exercises to keep them going!

  • Split squats
  • Walking lunges
  • Glute bridge
  • Calf raises
  • Box jump

    • Abs

    Human abdominal consist of four muscles which are the rectus abdomens, internal oblique, external oblique, and transversus abdominis. If you are new to ab exercises, these ones will be interesting! While they look simple enough, they can and will hurt very quickly, so be prepared!

  • Plank
  • Russian twist
  • Leg raise
  • Mountain Climbers
  • Flutter kicks
